# Relationship FAQ - Single & Dating

#### How to become more attractive to potential partners?
Attractiveness comes from both appearance and personality. Start with personal grooming, wear clothes that suit your style, and exercise regularly. Build inner qualities by developing hobbies like reading or learning an instrument. Actively participate in social activities to improve communication skills and project confidence. For example, someone who was once introverted started joining outdoor sports and book clubs, gradually becoming more outgoing and attracting genuine interest from others.

#### How to approach someone you're interested in at social events?
Keep a warm smile and look for natural conversation starters — for example, at an art exhibit, ask for their opinion on a piece. Express genuine interest sincerely, such as "You seem to have great insights on this, I'd love to hear more." Listen attentively and give positive feedback to keep the conversation flowing naturally.

#### Tips for online dating to increase success rate?
Create a genuine and appealing profile with clear photos that reflect your personality. Start conversations with light topics like hobbies or interesting daily experiences — avoid being too pushy early on. Maintain a balanced chat frequency and protect your privacy until you know someone better.

#### How to overcome anxiety about being single?
Acknowledge your anxiety and identify its root cause. Enrich your life by investing in work and hobbies to shift focus away from relationship pressure. Talk to close friends and family for support. For example, picking up photography and joining related activities can make life feel fuller and naturally reduce dating anxiety.

#### How to evaluate whether a blind date is worth pursuing?
Observe how they treat others (e.g., service staff). Understand their values around family, career, and lifestyle to see if they align with yours. Assess their emotional stability and whether they have clear life goals. A good sign is when both parties share similar visions for the future.
